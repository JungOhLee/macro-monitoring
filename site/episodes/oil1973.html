1973-74 oil-shock bear (peak: January 1973)

Type: inflation/regime-change bear on top of a narrow-valuation bubble. The case where the famous trigger (OPEC embargo) came nine months AFTER the peak - monetary regime change made the top; the oil shock made the bottom.

The setup

Institutions crowded into the Nifty Fifty "one-decision" growth stocks (Polaroid ~90x, McDonald's ~85x earnings) while the median stock lagged - a two-tier market. Underneath, the monetary anchor was dissolving: Nixon closed the gold window August 1971, the dollar was devalued twice (Dec 1971, Feb 1973), and Burns ran M2 at ~13% YoY into the 1972 election while wage-price controls bottled up the resulting inflation.

The causal chain

Election-year monetary ease + Bretton Woods collapse -> dollar devaluation, commodity and wage inflation -> controls loosened (Phase III, Jan 1973), CPI 3.4% -> 8.7% during 1973 -> Burns tightens hard (fed funds ~6% -> ~11% by Sep 1973) -> multiple compression guts the Nifty Fifty -> Oct 1973 embargo quadruples oil into an already-falling market -> stagflation -> earnings and multiples contract together.

The turn

The S&P peaked January 11, 1973 at 120.24 with no dramatic trigger - rate pressure plus the February dollar devaluation - and broke its 200-DMA within ~8% of the top by late February. The Yom Kippur War (Oct 6, 1973) and embargo (production cuts announced Oct 17; full US embargo on Oct 19-20) hit a market already down ~8.5%. The rout was not immediate - the S&P held near 110 until about Oct 26, then fell ~17% by early December. NBER recession from November 1973. Bottom October 3, 1974 at 62.28: -48% over 21 months, Nifty Fifty names down 70-90%.

What it looked like in the indicators

  • Valuation: CAPE ~18.7 at the peak (single digits at the bottom) - the excess was concentration, which aggregate CAPE and Buffett (~80% of GDP) understated.
  • ERP: negative at the peak (CAPE yield ~5.4% vs 10Y ~6.5%), far worse against realized inflation - a cleaner warning than the CAPE level.
  • Liquidity: M2 YoY decelerated from ~13% as CPI accelerated. Real fed funds were near or below zero through 1971-72 (the stimulative phase that set up the peak), stayed only modestly positive against accelerating inflation through 1973 despite the fastest tightening on record, then collapsed to zero-to-deeply-negative through 1974 (Dec 1974: 8.5% FF vs 12.1% CPI) - even the 12.9% July 1974 funds peak barely cleared 11.5% CPI. Burns stayed behind the curve throughout.
  • Curve/credit: bills out-yielded the 10Y from mid-1973 (10Y-2Y series only starts 1976); Baa spreads widened mostly through 1974 - confirming, not leading.
  • Data limits: no VIX, RSP/SPY, SLOOS, or margin-debt series here. Sahm rule triggered by mid-1974 - post-peak, pre-trough. CFNAI (1967+) readings turned negative through 1974, but its -0.7 MA3 recession threshold was crossed only at the October 1974 bottom - coincident, not leading.

Lessons for the monitor

Trend was the workhorse: the 200-DMA break came ~8% off the top and stayed bearish for most of two years. Aggregate valuation said little - the warning lived in the liquidity pillar (the negative real rates of 1971-72, before real fed funds turned positive into the top; decelerating real M2; the 1973 inversion). Regime change, not bubble metrics, carried the signal.
